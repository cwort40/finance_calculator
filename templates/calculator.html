How the Option Price Calculator Works
This calculator uses the Black-Scholes formula to calculate the price of an option, which is a contract that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified price before a certain date. The formula takes into account the current price of the asset, the strike price of the option, the time until the option expires, the risk-free rate, and the volatility of the underlying asset. The result is the theoretical fair value for the option.
